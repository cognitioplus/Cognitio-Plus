Gap Addressed: "BUILDING RESILIENCE" provides a more comprehensive and actionable guide beyond personal anecdotes to offer a structured framework and diverse strategies for building resilience.

 

      2. "Option B: Facing Adversity, Building Resilience, and Finding Joy" by Sheryl Sandberg and Adam Grant (2017)

Comparison: Both books delve into resilience in adversity. However, "BUILDING RESILIENCE" provides a broader perspective, incorporating elements of systems thinking, conflict-sensitive project management, and social innovation, making it more comprehensive in its approach.

Gap Addressed: "BUILDING RESILIENCE" offers a unique blend of principles and practical techniques to foster resilience, addressing personal resilience and its application in various contexts, such as disaster risk reduction and social enterprises.

       Comparison: Both books focus on inner strength and resilience. However, "BUILDING RESILIENCE" introduces a more contemporary perspective by emphasizing digital solutions, data analytics, and technology's role in resilience building.

       Gap Addressed: "BUILDING RESILIENCE" acknowledges the role of technology in today's world, providing insights on harnessing it for resilience, which is not as prominent in older publications.

Comparison: Both books explore resilience as a response to life's challenges. However, "BUILDING RESILIENCE" introduces the concept of conflict-sensitive project management and social innovations, offering a more comprehensive view of resilience.
